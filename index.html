Pasteles

Family Recipes and Culture

History of the recipe

Pasteles come from the indigenous people of Puerto Rico the Taino and originate before colonial times. The tainos made the first masa with cassava, yautia, and squash. The frying techniques and use of bananas in the recipe are likely from African Slaves who came from Africa to Puerto Rico. The sofrito used to make pasteles comes from Spain.Pasteles were influenced collectively from ingredients and techniques from the Indigenous, West African, Spanish people who inhabited the island . Pasteles became associated with Holidays because of the long process it took to make them. and how they are wrapped like presents.

Description

Pasteles are a traditional Puerto Rican food that are typically served on holidays such as Christmas and Three Kings Day. They are similar to tamales with many Latin American countries having similar versions with Hallacas which are from Venezuela being an example of this. Instead of corn husks which are used with tamales pasteles use banana leaves and are boiled rather than steamed. The masa comprises green plantations, white yautia, potato, and They are typically served with arroz con gandules, a Puerto Rican staple. Making pasteles is a family effort with many family members making different parts forming an assembly line.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 Pound of Vegan Meat or meat
  • Two teaspoons of green peppers
  • Two tablespoons of chopped red peppers
  • Two tablespoons of chopped celery
  • 1 Tablespoon of Ground Garlic
  • 1/2 can of tomato sauce
  • 20 green bananas
  • 2 to three yautia
  • 1 cup of pumpkin
  • 3 tablespoons of achiote oil
  • Two tablespoons banana leaves
  • Green plantations
  • 1 tablespoon of Kosher Salt
  • 1 tablespoon ground oregano
  • 1 to envelopes of Sazon
  • 1 to 2 cups of broth
  • 3 tablespoons of corn oil

>Directions

  1. Make the filling
  2. In a skillet brown either the vegan meat or meat with olive oil
  3. Add the peppers, chopped onion, garlic, and oregano together
  4. Make the masa dough
  5. Grate the plantations and yautia
  6. Blend the roots
  7. Leave the masa over a tablecloth for at least 3 hours to remove excess moisture
  8. Stir in the garlic, recaito, salt, sofrito, and achiote oil with the masa
  9. Wrap the pastales
  10. Prepare the banana leaves
  11. Brush achiote oil on each leaf
  12. Spread about 1 1/2 to 2 cups of masa on each leaf
  13. On top add a spoonful of filling
  14. Completely cover the filling with masa by bringing the edges of the leaf over the filling
  15. Put the edges together and fold down over the top
  16. Fold the edges of the leaf under
  17. Using parchment paper bring the top and bottom edges over the top and fold
  18. Tie the pastale with string in both directions
  19. Cooking
  20. Submerge the pastales in a pot of water and boil for an hour
  21. Remove from the water and cut the strings before serving
